Humans are never good at created genuinely good, random passwords. People often use words, numbers, or a combination that means something to them. This includes a pet's name, their mothers maiden name, their kids birthday, their anniversary... This leads to passwords that are really easy to guess.

You have to realise that humans are not the ones that are guessing the password. When someone is trying to get into your account they use a computer to guess the password. An ordinary desktop can test over a hundered million passwords per second. If they use a computer with some kind of GPU-based cracking tools then this number climbs billions per second. the length and complexity of a password is crucial to have a truly secure account.

A truly good password is long, like really long. You have to think like over 16 characters that uses upper and lower case letters, numbers, and special characters in a random order. The only issue here is that humans aren't good at remembering good passwords like that. Thats why you should have all the passwords either written down somewhere secure or use a third party service.
